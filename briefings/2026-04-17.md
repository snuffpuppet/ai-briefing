**References:** No specific studies or named sources cited in the available extract. The argument is structural/first-principles.

**Justifications:** The strongest point is the conceptual distinction between prompt-level AI (task-isolated, no learning) and operating-layer AI (feedback loops, governance, accumulated policy from exceptions and corrections). The weakest point is the absence of concrete case evidence — the argument is by reasoning rather than example.

**Core points:**
- The operating layer is defined as: instrumented operations + feedback loops from human decisions + governance that turns exceptions into policy
- Advantage compounds: each correction, approval, or exception is a learning signal

**Impact:** A practical frame for any team scoping enterprise AI integration. For solutions architecture in OSS platforms, the operating layer concept maps directly to: instrumenting existing workflows to generate feedback signals, ensuring governance closes the loop (not just audits outputs), and designing for accumulated intelligence rather than per-query utility.
