Dynamic Workflows is the same orchestration substrate the Bun rewrite depended on; it's worth watching as it moves toward GA.

### 2. AI reasoning models are vulnerable to deliberate overthinking attacks

**Source:** IEEE Spectrum · **Published:** 2026-07-08 · **Read time:** ~7 min

**Elevator pitch.** Adversarial inputs designed to trigger excessive chain-of-thought in reasoning models can degrade service quality, raise costs, and cause response timeouts—without producing incorrect answers that would trip conventional safety filters.

**Opinion vs. fact**
- [FACT] Researchers from Zhejiang University and Alibaba presented findings at ICML 2026 in Seoul showing reasoning models can be forced into excessive reasoning via crafted inputs.
- [FACT] The attack works by injecting bait reasoning tasks—Markov decision processes, Sudoku puzzles—into the model's context.
- [FACT] The underlying paper, "OverThink: Slowdown Attacks on Reasoning LLMs" (arxiv 2502.02542), was published February 2025 and has since been extended.
- [FACT] A black-box variant exists: "Inducing Overthink: Hierarchical Genetic Algorithm-based DoS Attack on Black-Box Large Language Reasoning Models" (arxiv 2605.13338).
- [OPINION] The IEEE Spectrum article characterises this as a "critical vulnerability"—actual severity depends on deployment context and access controls.

**References.** Zhejiang University; Alibaba; ICML 2026 (Seoul); arxiv 2502.02542 (OverThink); arxiv 2605.13338 (Inducing Overthink); IEEE Spectrum.

**Justifications.** Strongest: the attack has peer-reviewed research behind it; an ICML presentation adds credibility and reproducibility. The existence of a black-box variant means it's testable without access to model internals. Weakest: the article doesn't quantify success rate across different reasoning model configurations or specify how much overhead an attacker must invest.

**Core points**
- The attack exploits extended internal reasoning: crafted inputs trigger unnecessary extra thinking without changing output correctness, making it invisible to output-based filters.
- Effect on production systems: elevated token consumption, slower inference, response timeouts—a cost and availability attack, not a correctness attack.
- Two attack variants: white-box (requires internal access) and black-box (genetic algorithm—usable against any API endpoint).
- Proposed mitigations include token budgets and reasoning length caps, but these trade off against model performance on legitimately complex queries.
- Attack surface grows as reasoning models (o3, Fable 5, Opus 4.8, Grok 4.5) become more widely deployed in production APIs.

**Impact.** For anyone deploying reasoning models in multi-tenant APIs or agentic pipelines where inputs arrive from untrusted sources, this attack class is operationally relevant now. The key design implication: reasoning length should be bounded at the infrastructure level for production deployments, not left to the model to self-regulate. Cost anomaly monitoring—tracking token spend per request and alerting on outliers—should be part of any production observability setup. This is not a theoretical risk; it was demonstrated at ICML 2026 on current production models.
